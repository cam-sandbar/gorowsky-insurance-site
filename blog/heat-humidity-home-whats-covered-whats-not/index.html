Heat, Humidity, and Your Home: What Is Covered and What Is Not

Home › Blog › Heat, Humidity, and Your Home: What Is Covered and What Is Not

By Glen Gorowsky  |  March 7, 2026  |  Home Insurance

If you live along the Gulf Coast in Baldwin County or Mobile County, you already know that heat and humidity are a way of life. Summers regularly bring temperatures in the 90s with humidity levels that make it feel well over 100 degrees. In communities like Daphne and across coastal Alabama, that combination can do real damage to your home over time. Understanding what your homeowners policy covers, and what it does not, can save you significant money and frustration.

What Humidity and Heat Damage Is Typically NOT Covered

This is the part most homeowners do not know until after a claim is denied. Standard homeowners policies exclude damage caused by gradual wear and tear, lack of maintenance, and long-term moisture intrusion. That means the following scenarios are almost always excluded:

  • Mold growth that develops gradually over time due to poor ventilation or high humidity
  • Wood rot on siding, decking, or framing caused by chronic moisture exposure
  • HVAC failure caused by deferred maintenance or age-related wear
  • Foundation cracking or settlement caused by soil movement from repeated wet and dry cycles
  • Pest damage that occurs because humidity attracted termites or other wood-destroying insects

What IS Covered Under a Standard Policy

The key distinction is sudden and accidental damage versus gradual deterioration. Covered scenarios related to heat and humidity typically include:

  • Sudden pipe burst caused by a pressure failure, even in summer when thermal expansion plays a role
  • Accidental water damage from a broken appliance, not slow leak-related damage
  • Storm-related damage from a severe thunderstorm, tornado, or hurricane even when humidity was a contributing factor
  • Fire damage including damage from a lightning strike

Coverage Add-Ons Worth Considering

Two endorsements are especially relevant for Baldwin County and Gulf Coast homeowners dealing with humidity-related risks. Water backup coverage protects against damage from sewer or drain backup, which is increasingly common during heavy rainfall events. Equipment breakdown coverage protects your HVAC system and major appliances from sudden mechanical failure, including electrical breakdowns from power fluctuations during summer storms.

How to Reduce Your Risk and Protect Your Coverage

The best defense against a denied humidity-related claim is documented maintenance. Keep records of HVAC service calls, roof inspections, and any moisture remediation work. Carriers are much more likely to support a claim when the homeowner can demonstrate they maintained the property reasonably.
